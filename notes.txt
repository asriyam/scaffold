Light reads 

Starting off my light reading phase with PG Woodhouse. This will set me up for a long reading spell I guess. This is also my first book of PG Woodhouse. A long time back, I happen to listen to an audio version of PG Woodhouse and the English repelled me. So I never picked Woodhouse. A friend recommended me this book "Carry On, Jeeves" as a start and advised me to just the let the language wash over and I will get it. 

This book "Carry On, Jeeves" is a collection of short stories. I just read first two "Jeeves takes charge" and "The artistic career of Corky" - both are very humorous and enjoyable. The pair Bertie Wooster and Jeeves and their funny interactions - not a new concept to me - most comedy I have read or watched as movies involve two people, one a bit inferior in intellect, make funny interesting conversations. There is still seven or eight stories to read in this book and it should be a breeze.
